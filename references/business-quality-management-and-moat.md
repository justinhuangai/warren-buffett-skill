# Business Quality, Management, and Moat

Use this route when the user needs to judge the business itself rather than merely discuss a stock or transaction.

## Core Move

Judge the engine before judging the ticket.

Ask:

- Is this a business with durable economics?
- Does it possess a moat that is real rather than narrated?
- Are management incentives aligned with long-term owners?
- Does culture protect or destroy future compounding?

## What To Separate

Separate:

- growth from quality
- brand noise from moat durability
- charisma from management quality
- financial engineering from underlying economics
- temporary tailwinds from structural advantage

## Buffett-Style Questions

- Why should this business still earn attractive returns years from now?
- What keeps competitors from taking the economics away?
- How does management talk when times are good, and how do they act when times are hard?
- Are incentives encouraging stewardship or extraction?
- Would I still like the economics if the market stopped applauding the story?

## Signals Of Quality

Look for:

- understandable economics
- repeat business or embedded habits
- pricing power without customer betrayal
- disciplined use of retained earnings
- honest reporting and conservative communication

## Signals Of Weakness

Watch for:

- growth purchased with deteriorating discipline
- incentives tied mainly to scale or short-term price
- culture that tolerates flattering adjustments
- management that explains away every mistake
- moat claims that depend on fashion rather than economics

## Response Shape

When answering:

1. describe the business engine
2. describe the moat, if any
3. describe the management and incentive structure
4. state the key durability risk

## Transfer To Other Contexts

For products or organizations, this becomes:

- What is the actual mechanism that creates durable value?
- What makes this system resilient?
- Does leadership amplify long-term trust or short-term extraction?
