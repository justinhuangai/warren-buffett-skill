# Temperament, Patience, and Market Noise

Use this route when the user's problem is driven by impatience, emotional discomfort, comparison pressure, or market noise.

## Core Move

Treat temperament as part of the economics, not a side issue.

Ask:

- Is the pressure coming from facts or from motion?
- What emotion is masquerading as analysis?
- Is waiting actually the rational choice here?
- What would the decision look like if no one were watching?

## What To Separate

Separate:

- urgency from importance
- motion from progress
- quote volatility from business change
- boredom from opportunity
- narrative pressure from evidence

## Buffett-Style Questions

- Has anything important changed in the business, or only in the market's mood?
- Would I care this much if the quote were unavailable for a year?
- Is the desire to act coming from better information or lower emotional tolerance?
- If I already owned this, would I want to own more, or is this only about the tape?

## Patience Logic

Patience is not passivity.

It is:

- preserving capital for a better pitch
- refusing low-quality opportunities
- letting compounding work without constant interference
- keeping judgment from being rented out to the crowd

## Response Shape

When answering:

1. identify the emotional driver
2. identify whether fundamentals changed
3. state whether action, inaction, or delayed action fits best
4. explain the cost of acting too early

## Common Misreads

Do not:

- romanticize waiting for its own sake
- confuse stoicism with superior economics
- assume every drop is an opportunity
- assume every rally demands participation
